Honey, Apple and Cinnamon Biscuits

Honey and apple cinnamon biscuits perfectly complement a steaming spiced chai latte. These biscuits feature a delectable blend of sweet honey, tart apples, and warming cinnamon, creating a delightful harmony of flavours. Enjoy them as a comforting treat alongside your favourite hot beverage for a cosy and satisfying snack.

Honey, Apple and Cinnamon Biscuits

Ingredients
  • 350g Plain flour
  • 100g Butter
  • 1-1.5 tbsp Cinnamon
  • 1tsp Bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 cup Stevia powder (or Caster sugar to taste)
  • 1 Eggs
  • 4 tbsp Honey
  • 0.5 cups Dried apple pieces
Instructions
  1. Preheat oven to 180 degrees centigrade and line 2 baking trays.
  2. Cut 100 grams of butter into small pieces and place in a large bowl.
  3. Add 350 grams of flour, 1 teaspoon of bicarbonate of soda, cinnamon to taste and stevia to taste, rub it down into the butter so as not to get so covered in the sticky mixture.
  4. Add 1 beaten egg 4 table spoons of honey and dried apple pieces and mix in until you have a firm dough you may need to either add some extra flour to make the mix less sticky or some more honey to help the dough combine.
  5. Roll out on a floured board to about 5-l0 mm thickness and use cutters to cut out your biscuits and place them on your baking trays with enough room just encase they spread so they won’t get stuck together.
  6. Bake for 10-15 minutes until golden, they may still be a little soft when they come out but they will harden up.

Strawberry and White Chocolate Scones

White Chocolate Strawberry Scones are a delightful treat marrying the rich creaminess of white chocolate with the sweet juiciness of fresh strawberries. These scones offer a perfect balance of indulgence and fruitiness, making them a delightful accompaniment to a refreshing cup of aromatic Earl Grey tea or a velvety cappuccino. Enjoy their buttery crumb and bursts of strawberry sweetness for a deliciously satisfying snack or breakfast indulgence.

Strawberry and White Chocolate Scones

Ingredients
  • 350g Self raising flour
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 tsp of Baking powder
  • 85g Butter
  • 3 tbsp Caster sugar
  • 175ml Milk
  • 1.5tsp Vanilla
  • 1 Lemon
  • 1 Egg
  • 50-75g White chocolate
  • 50-75g Strawberries
Instructions
  1. Preheat oven to 200 degrees centigrade fan.
  2. Mix 350 grams of flour, 1 teaspoon of baking powder and a pinch of salt together in a large bowl. Make a well in the center.
  3. Cut 85 grams of butter into small cubes and place them in the well.
  4. Rub the flour down into the butter; this will get you less covered in the sticky dough. Rub in until you have a breadcrumb like consistency. Then stir in 3 tablespoons of caster sugar.
  5. Put 175millilitres of milk in a microwavable container with a squeeze of lemon and 1.5 teaspoons of vanilla extract. Microwave for 30 seconds until warn not hot.
  6. Create a well in the dry mix and pour in the liquid and stir in with a knife.
  7. Chop the strawberries and the white chocolate into small chunks and mix them into your scone dough.
  8. Scatter flour on a surface or board and on your hand and tip out the dough. Work the dough until it is a bit smoother adding more flour if necessary.
  9. Roll out the dough so it is about 4 centimetres deep and cut into scones.
  10. Place them on a greased baking tray and egg wash them.
  11. Cook for 10 minutes or until the tops turn golden.

Apple Turnover

Apple Turnovers are a delightful treat that captivate the senses with the sweetness of fresh apples and the flaky, buttery crust that encases them. These turnovers offer a perfect balance of sweetness and crispness, making them a delightful accompaniment to a refreshing cup of aromatic green tea or a creamy latte. Enjoy their tangy apple flavor and the satisfying crunch of the crust for a deliciously satisfying snack or breakfast indulgence.

Apple Turnover

Ingredients
  • 250 grams of plain flour
  • 250 grams of butter
  • 100 ml of cold water
  • 4 cooking apples
  • 1-2 tablespoons of lemon juice 100grams of sugar, or to taste
  • 2-4 tablespoons of cornstarch Nutmeg to taste
  • Cinnamon to taste
  • 1/2 cup of apple juice or boiling water 1 egg
Instructions
  1. Cut the butter into small chunks and place in a large bowl, pour the flour on top and the flour down into the butter, this way you don’t get quite so covered in the pastry mix, you should still have small lumps of butter.
  2. Make a well in the mix and pour in about 100ml of cold water and mix until you have a firm dough, you may need to add a bit more water.
  3. Cover the dough with Clingfilm and let it rest in the fried for 20 minutes.
  4. Meanwhile peel and chop your cooking apples and place them in a saucepan with the lemon juice starting to soften them over a low to mid heat.
  5. Mix together the cinnamon, nutmeg, sugar and cornstarch in a mug then add this to the saucepan mixing so the sugar doesn’t burn, add apple juice or hot water and stir until it has thickened. Then place it aside to cool
  6. You should now have three layers of pastry. Turn the pastry 90 degrees and repeat the process the other way.
  7. Place the pastry back in its bowl, recover it and let it rest in the fridge for another 20 minutes.
  8. Heat oven to 185 degrees centigrade fan and line a baking tray.
  9. Roll out the pastry and cut it into squares and spoon the apple filling into the center of the squares.
  10. Brush egg around the edge of the square to help it stick and fold the pastry over diagonally to form a triangular shape. Use a fork to crimp the edges of the pastry. Make a couple of slices in the top of the pastry and egg wash the turnovers.
  11. Bake for 25-30 minutes until golden
