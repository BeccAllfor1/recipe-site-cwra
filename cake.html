Coffee Cupcakes

Savour coffee cupcakes, where rich espresso flavours mingle with moist cake. Each bite is a caffeinated delight, perfect with a hot cup of freshly brewed coffee for a harmonious indulgence.

Coffee Cupcakes

Ingredients
  • 115g Butter
  • 115g Caster sugar
  • 115g Self raising flour
  • 1tsp Baking powder
  • 2 Eggs
  • 1/2 Cup of strong coffee
Instructions
  1. Preheat your oven to 200 degrees centigrade fan, and line up ten cupcake cases in a cupcake tray.
  2. Place 115 grams of butter in a microwavable bowl and microwave until it is liquid.
  3. Whisk the liquid butter to re combine it then whisk in 115 grams of caster sugar.
  4. Mix 115 grams of self rising flour and 1 teaspoon baking powder together with a spoon in a separate bowl.
  5. Add 1 beaten egg and 1/3 of the flour mix to the butter and whisk well. Repeat this one more time. You should be left with about 1/3 of the flour mix.
  6. Add 1/3 a cup of coffee and the rest of the flour and mix well.
  7. Add more coffee if necessary, it should be a fairly wet mix but not too wet.
  8. Split equally between the cupcake cases and cook for 15-20 minutes or until when you stick a carving fork in it comes out clean.
  9. Leave the cakes to cool.
  10. Mix some icing sugar with coffee to make an icing that is thick enough that it won’t run everywhere and spread it over your cupcakes. and decorate to taste.

Lemon Traybake

Delight in a Lemon Traybake, where zesty lemon infuses every bite of moist cake. With each forkful, a burst of citrusy freshness dances on your palate. Pair it with a refreshing glass of iced lemonade for a harmonious combination that's both bright and invigorating.

Lemon Cake

Ingredients
  • 275g Self raising flour
  • 225g Butter
  • 225g Caster sugar
  • 4 Eggs
  • 2tsp Baking powder
  • 2 Lemons
  • Icing sugar
Instructions
  1. Preheat oven to 180 degrees centigrade fan and line a baking tray
  2. Place 225 grams of butter in a microwaveable bowl and melt in the microwave.
  3. Whisk melted butter well to recombine then add 225 grams of caster and whisk vigorously.
  4. Mix together in a separate bowl 275 grams of self raising flour and 2 teaspoons of baking powder.
  5. Add one beaten egg and 1/5 of the flour mix into the butter and whisk thoroughly, repeat three more times you should be left with 1/5 of the flour.
  6. Add the zest and juice of 1 lemon and the end of the flour and whisk thoroughly.
  7. Pour the cake mix into the prepared baking tray making sure it-Ls evenly distributed and cook for 30-35 minutes or until a carving fork comes out clean when you test it.
  8. Meanwhile place 3-4 tablespoons of icing sugar into a bowl, add enough lemon juice to make a very wet icing and stir until it is combined.
  9. When the cake is done and has just come out of the oven pierce the top of it with a fork at a fairly even distance from each other and spread the wet drizzle icing over the top to sink in.
  10. Once the cake is cool mix some more icing sugar with lemon juice to make a thicker icing that won’t run everywhere and spread it over your tray bake.

Chocolate Orange Cake

Indulge in a slice of chocolate orange cake, where rich cocoa meets zesty orange in a decadent fusion. With each bite, velvety chocolate melts in your mouth, intertwined with vibrant citrus notes. Enjoy it alongside a smooth cup of hot chocolate for a perfect pairing that balances the richness of chocolate with the tangy brightness of orange, creating a harmonious symphony of flavors.

Chocolate Orange Cake

Ingredients
  • 225g Self raising flour
  • 225g Caster sugar
  • 225g Butter
  • 3-4 tbsp Cocoa powder
  • 1-1.5 tsp Baking powder
  • 2 cups Orange juice
  • 4 Eggs
  • Basic chocolate buttercream icing
  • Icing sugar
Instructions
  1. Preheat your oven to 200 degrees centigrade fan, and line two cake tins.
  2. Place 225 grams of butter in a microwavable bowl and microwave until it is liquid.
  3. Whisk the liquid butter to re combine it then whisk in 225 grams of caster sugar.
  4. Mix 225 grams of self rising flour, cocoa powder and baking powder together with a spoon in a separate bowl.
  5. Add 1 beaten egg and 1/5 of the flour mix to the butter and whisk well. Repeat this three more times you should be left with about 1/5 of the flour mix.
  6. Add 1/2a cup of orange juice and the rest of the flour and mix well. Add more orange juice if necessary, it should be a fairly wet mix but soupy.
  7. Split equally between two tins and cook for about 30 minutes or until when you stick a carving fork in it comes out clean.
  8. Leave the cake to cool.
  9. In a bowl place 4 table spoons of chocolate icing, icing sugar and just enough orange juice to get a nice thick creamy chocolate orange icing. Icing sugar and orange juice to taste as some people will like it orangeier than others.
  10. Once the cake is cooled spread icing over one layer. Place the second on top and ice the top as well.
  11. If you have any leftover icing you can make a nice orange sauce to go with your cake by just adding more orange just and mixing well.
